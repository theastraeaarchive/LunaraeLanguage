Everyday and Sacred Expressions in Lunarae

Phrases in Lunarae are meant to be breathed, not merely spoken. Use the breath mark ~ to end gently. Remember: descriptors follow nouns, and particles carry state and relation.

Greetings & Farewells

  • Silen alae~ — I see the quiet light in you.
  • Alae silen~ — And I remember it in myself.
  • Ahalon alae~ — I recognize you (soulfully).
  • Lunasil mura~ — Return to your star-lake (rest well).
  • Silen mura~ — Return softly.
  • Noa luae~ — Peace to us / among us.

Everyday Use

  • Ene la va lua~ — I see the light.
  • Nea la saen noa~ — You speak peace.
  • Lua vaya vani~ — The light shines beautifully.
  • Na mura ae lona noa~ — I will rest in the peaceful home.
  • Ae la lira vani? — Do you feel beauty?
  • Ene en aeni silen~ — I remembered stillness.
  • Ene la sa va lua~ — I do not see the light.
  • Noa luae~ — Peace with us / among us.

Emotional Language (Verb-less Forms)

  • Silaren ene~ — I am longing.
  • Lirune luae~ — We are connected (with empathy).
  • Elnara ene~ — I am clear/settled after feeling.
  • Veyar ene~ — I am in quiet wonder.
  • Athein ene~ — I am steady in my light.
  • Nuvah ene~ — I am renewing / beginning again.

Care & Support

  • Ene la lira nea~ — I feel you / I’m with you.
  • Noa nea~ — Peace to you.
  • Mura, mura~ — Rest, deeply.
  • Ae na noan luae? — Shall we find peace together?
  • Halu la nea~ — Breathe, you are here.
  • Silen ae ene~ — Be still with yourself.

Blessings & Intentions

  • Lua vani ae raen nea~ — May beauty-light guide your path.
  • Athein alae nea~ — I honor your steady light.
  • Lunasil alae nea~ — I see the star-lake in you.
  • Noa ae lona nea~ — Peace to your home.
  • Nuvah ae ene~ — Renewal to your being.

Questions (Soft Queries)

  • Ae la va lua? — Do I/you see the light?
  • Ae en aeni lunasil? — Did you remember the star-lake?
  • Ae na mura ae lona? — Will you rest at home?
  • Ae la lira silaren? — Are you feeling longing?
  • Ae la saen noa? — Are you speaking peace?

Tiny Dialogues

— Alae silen~

— Ae la lira silaren?

— “Are you feeling longing?”

Build-Your-Own (Patterns)

Swap in any noun/verb/descriptor while keeping particles and flow:

  • Ene [state] [verb] [noun] [descriptor]~
    I [am/will/remember] [verb] [thing] [quality].
    e.g., Ene la va lua raeli~ — I see radiant light.
  • [Emotion] [pronoun]~
    e.g., Lirune luae~ — We are connected.
  • [Noun] ae [noun]~
    Relationship/belonging. e.g., Lua ae lona~ — Light of home.

Notes on Breath & Tone

Use a softer voice at phrase endings. The ~ breath mark signals a gentle finish. For blessing or recognition, elongate vowels slightly: “Alae…” For clarity (Elnara), keep rhythm even and unhurried.
